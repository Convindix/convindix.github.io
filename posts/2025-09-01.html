You can't embed a cube into a square poset

Let $P$ be the poset which is the powerset of $\{0,1,2\}$ ordered by inclusion, and for a natural number let $Q_n$ be the poset whose underlying set is $\{0,1,\ldots,n-1\}$ ordered by componentwise comparison, i.e. the relation $(a_1,b_1)\leq(a_2,b_2)$ iff $a_1\leq a_2$ and $b_1\leq b_2$. The posets $P$ and $Q_n$ can be visualized as a $2\times 2\times 2$ cube and an $n\times n$ square respectively. Below are Hasse diagrams (as directed graphs) for $P$ and $Q_4$.

Is there an $n$ such that there is an order preserving injection of $P$ into $Q_n$?

It turns out that the answer is no.

The notion of the order dimension of a poset $(R,\prec)$ has two equivalent definitions: (see G. Bergman, "Some Frustrating Questions on Dimensions of Products of Posets", 2025, definition 1.1)

  • The smallest $n$ such that there is a set of $n$ linear extensions of $(R,\prec)$ on $R$ whose intersection is $(R,\prec)$,
  • The smallest $n$ such that $R$ is embeddable into a Cartesian product of $n$ linearly orderable posets with the componentwise ordering.

The order dimension of $P$ is $3$ (see Bergman, inequality 1.7), so it cannot embed into $Q_n$ for any natural number $n$, since each of those posets $Q_n$ is the Cartesian product of two linearly ordered sets $\{0,1,\ldots,n-1\}$.

This has implications in some of my attempts to get a model-theoretic proof of independence of the finite adjacent Ramsey theorem. In particular, some of the hypothetical functions which I had been hoping would code truth of formulas into a lower-dimensional space will have to be non-bijective.
