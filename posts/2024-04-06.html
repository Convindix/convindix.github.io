Jeon and Walsh's preprint "Generalized ordinal analysis and reflection principles in set theory" contains some work which reminds me of Rathjen and Möllerfeld's note "A note on the $\Sigma_1$ spectrum of a theory" (open-access version). One of the key differences is that the ordinals $\beta$ considered for $\vert T\vert_{\boldsymbol{\alpha}\text{-repr}}$ must have $T\vdash\mathsf{Exists}(\boldsymbol{\beta})^{L_\alpha}$, whereas Rathjen and Möllerfeld only require that $L\vDash\exist\xi\phi(\xi)$.
