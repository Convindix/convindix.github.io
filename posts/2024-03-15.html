Another connection between PA and ZFC

Let $\mathrm{ZF}\lnot\mathrm{Inf}$ be the theory $\mathrm{ZF}$ but with the axiom of infinity negated. It is already well-known that $\mathrm{PA}$ and $\mathrm{ZF}\lnot\mathrm{Inf}$ are mutually interpretable. (A strictly stronger result which needs some clarification is described as folklore in Kaye and Wong's "On interpretations of arithmetic and set theory", 2007.) It is also known (ibid.) that fragments of $\mathrm{ZF}\lnot\mathrm{Inf}$ (when axiomaitzed with $\in$induction) and fragments of $\mathrm{PA}$ are mutually interpretable.

There is a theorem of Montague and Vaught that if $\kappa$ is such that there is an ordinal $\alpha>\kappa$ such that $V_\alpha$ is an elementary extension of $V_\kappa$, then $V_\kappa$ is a model of $\mathrm{ZF}$ (Marek, "Stable sets, a characterization of $\beta_2$-models of full second order arithmetic and some related facts" p.186). (Note: this implication is the other direction from the next one.) On the side of arithmetic, the MacDowell-Specker theorem states that every model of $\mathrm{PA}$ has a nontrivial elementary end-extension. When looking at fragments of $\mathrm{PA}$, it has been shown that every model of $\mathsf I\Sigma_n$ has a nontrivial $\Sigma^0_n$-elementary end extension (Kossak, "63 years of the MacDowell-Specker theorem", 2022, p.5). Here we prove an analogous theorem for $\mathrm{ZF}$, that if $V_\kappa$ models $\mathrm{ZF}_n$, then there is an ordinal $\alpha>\kappa$ such that $V_\kappa\prec_{\Sigma_n}V_\alpha$.

Theorem 1: If there is an ordinal $\alpha>\kappa$ such that $V_\kappa\prec_{\Sigma_n}V_\alpha$, then $V_\kappa$ models $\mathrm{ZF}_n$. (Other direction)
